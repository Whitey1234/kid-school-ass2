Standard Curriculum

Kinder (3-6) Years

The Kinder age group, typically ranging from 3 to 6 years old, is a crucial period in a child’s development. This stage is often referred to as the preschool years or early childhood, and it bridges the gap between toddlerhood and primary school. During this time, children experience rapid growth in various areas, including cognitive, emotional, social, and physical development.

Elementary School

Elementary school, also known as primary school, is the foundational stage of formal education for children typically aged 6 to 11 years. This period is marked by significant growth in academic, social, and emotional development as children transition from early childhood to a more structured learning environment.

Middle (10-16) Years

The middle years, typically covering ages 10 to 16, represent a transformative phase in a child’s life. This period encompasses the later stages of childhood, early adolescence, and the beginning of teenage years. It is marked by significant physical, emotional, cognitive, and social changes as children transition from elementary school to middle and high school.
